What This Dashboard Explores

Between 2011 and 2023, Australia's recorded music industry went through one of the most dramatic transformations in its history. Physical formats like CDs and digital downloads collapsed, while streaming rose from almost nothing to dominate nearly 90% of all revenue. This dashboard tells that story through two lenses: how the money moved between formats inside Australia, and how Australia's position shifted on the global stage.

01
2011, The Old Guard
CDs still hold 58% of revenue. Downloads are rising. Streaming barely exists at $5M. Australia ranks 6th globally.
02
2013, The Tipping Point
Streaming jumps 5x to $52M. Downloads peak and begin their decline. Ringtones start disappearing. The collapse accelerates.
03
2015, Rock Bottom
Total revenue hits its lowest point at $334M. The industry hasn't figured out how to monetise digital yet. But streaming is growing fast.
04
2017, Streaming Takes Over
Streaming surges to $350M and becomes the dominant format. Total revenue recovers past $450M. The rebirth begins.
05
2023, New Heights and New Problems
Revenue hits $676M, an all-time high. Streaming is 89% of the market. But globally, Australia has slipped out of the top 10, overtaken by Asia.

Falling Behind

While formats shifted inside Australia, the country's global ranking was quietly slipping. Once the world's 6th-largest music market, Australia was overtaken by South Korea, China, and Brazil, dropping out of the top 10 entirely by 2024.
